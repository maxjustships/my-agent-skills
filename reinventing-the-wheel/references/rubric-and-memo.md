# Rubric and Memo

Use this reference to keep the final output consistent and auditable.

## Rubric

Score each dimension from `1` to `5`.

Interpret the numbers loosely:
- `1`: strongly unfavorable for a new entrant
- `3`: mixed or unclear
- `5`: strongly favorable for a new entrant

Dimensions:
- `market saturation`: how crowded the category is with credible alternatives
- `solution quality`: how strong the current options appear in product quality, UX, completeness, and trust
- `user pain`: how much unresolved pain, dissatisfaction, or demand is visible
- `differentiation headroom`: how much room exists to win on UX, quality, scope, speed, cost, workflow fit, or positioning
- `distribution difficulty`: how hard adoption appears because of incumbency, SEO, switching costs, or buyer behavior
- `build leverage`: how much leverage the idea creates for builders, teams, or businesses even if the market is crowded

Use the scores as the main evidence surface. Do not apply a rigid formula mechanically; explain any mixed cases.

## Recommendation Guidance

Allowed recommendations:
- `build`: the space still has meaningful room and a credible path to value
- `pivot`: the current framing is weak, but the underlying problem or capability suggests a better adjacent direction
- `niche down`: keep the idea but narrow the scope, user, or workflow
- `go vertical`: tailor the idea to a specific industry, workflow, or audience
- `drop`: the current idea looks weak even after testing a contrarian angle

Confidence bands:
- `low`: evidence is thin, mixed, or highly noisy
- `medium`: evidence is decent but not overwhelming
- `high`: evidence is strong and points in one direction

Before `drop`, always include one contrarian angle. If it is weak, say that it is low-confidence and still recommend `drop`.
